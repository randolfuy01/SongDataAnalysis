The 1975

Artists: Matty Healy, Ross MacDonald, George Daniel, Adam Hann

Music Type: Alternative Pop

From: Wilmslow, United Kingdom

Popularity index: 85

The 1975 is a British pop rock band known for their unique and genre-blending sound. Formed in 2002, the original members included Matthew Healy, Adam Hann, George Daniel, and Ross MacDonald. They gained prominence with their self-titled debut album in 2013, featuring hit singles like "Sex" and "Chocolate." The band's music defies easy categorization, drawing from pop, rock, electronic, and R&B influences. In subsequent years, The 1975 released several albums, including "I Like It When You Sleep, for You Are So Beautiful yet So Unaware of It" in 2016 and "A Brief Inquiry into Online Relationships" in 2018. Their music explores themes of modern life, technology, and relationships. Beyond their music, the band is known for visually captivating music videos and thought-provoking lyrics, earning them critical acclaim and a dedicated fan base.
